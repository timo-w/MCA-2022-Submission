    Comparing Analysis Methods

    Describe at least one advantage of a time-frequency analysis over a waveform-based analysis. Provide at least one example of the identified advantage by referencing a specific subpart of the previous output.

    Where waveforms can represent the shape, form, and magnitude of an audio wave at a given point, time-frequency representations can reveal the harmonic structure of a sound, as well as exactly what frequencies these occur in. One possible observation could be the specific frequencies or notes that are most prominent in a given time frame (like what chord is playing at a given bar of music). From this, the key of the piece could be deduced from identifying the most common notes.

    Another advantage of time-frequency analysis is the ability to more clearly distinguish the rhythmic qualities of a section of music.
